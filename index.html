Caesar Cipher (Classical Cryptography)



    
    

        
How the Caesar Cipher Works

        

            This cipher shifts each letter forward or backward by a fixed amount. 
            It’s one of the earliest known ciphers, used by Julius Caesar.
        


        

        

    







    
SHA‑256 Hash (One‑Way Hashing)


    
    

    


    
    

        
Understanding SHA‑256

        

            SHA‑256 is a modern cryptographic hash function. 
            It is one-way, meaning the input cannot be recovered from the output.
        


        

        

    







    
AES‑256 Encryption (Modern Symmetric Crypto)


    
    

    
    

    


    
    

        
How AES‑256 Works

        

            AES is the encryption standard used worldwide, from HTTPS traffic to 
            encrypted storage. AES‑256 uses a 256‑bit key and 14 rounds of transformation.
